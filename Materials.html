Materials and Shaders

In the past, materials were very different to shaders. Materials involved replicating the material of a real world object, and shaders were more involved with how light would interact with the materia. At this point in time it is more common to have both integrated together. This can cause confusion from time to time, but for the most part the terms are used interchangeibly. I will use the term 'Shader' for digital, and 'Material' for real world materials

There are two general forms of shader:

  • Procedural
  • Mapped / Image based

Procedural Shaders

Procedural Shaders are written in code that the rendering engine can understand.

  • Very flexible, infinite scale, infinite resolution
  • Infinite editability, if you know how
  • Can be specific to a rendering engine, so not portable from system to system
  • The Autodesk Material Library is procedural
